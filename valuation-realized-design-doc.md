## Problem Statement

SME founders/owners ($2-50M revenue) planning an exit in 6-18 months face a devastating information asymmetry: buyers and investors run hundreds of deals and systematically apply valuation haircuts during due diligence. Founders typically make 1-2 exits in their entire career and don't know what's coming. The result: a preventable 30% valuation haircut that the founder discovers too late to fix.

Big Four firms (EY, Deloitte, PwC, KPMG) offer sell-side preparation, but at $90K+ minimum — prohibitive for an SME. Local accountants and business brokers lack the institutional deal experience to identify and pre-fix the issues a sophisticated buyer will exploit. This leaves a massive gap: **Big Four quality exit preparation at an accessible price point.**

## Demand Evidence

**One real sale (accidental):** At a networking event, a founder asked for help valuing his company. EY quoted $90K minimum. Vaiga offered a personal $2K mini-valuation — and closed the deal on the spot. She had never done a standalone valuation before; the EY credential alone was enough to close.

**Key signal:** The founder sought her out. She didn't pitch. Demand pulled her — she didn't push the product.

**Price arbitrage:** 45x gap between Big Four ($90K) and what an independent Big Four alumna can charge ($2K). Even at $8-15K for a full exit readiness program, the value proposition is 6-10x cheaper than Big Four with the same caliber expertise.

**Information asymmetry is structural:** Investors make 100s of deals; founders make 1-2 in their lifetime. This asymmetry doesn't go away. Every SME exit is a repeat of the same pattern — unprepared seller, sophisticated buyer, preventable value destruction.

**Honest gap:** Vaiga has spent 8 years on the buyer/advisor side at EY-Parthenon. She knows exactly what destroys valuations in due diligence. But she hasn't yet mapped the SME seller's current journey in detail — what they google, who they call first, how they decide to hire help.

## Target User & Narrowest Wedge

**Target user:** Founder, owner, or CEO/CFO of an SME generating $2-50M annual revenue, planning to exit in 6-18 months. They don't yet know that a buyer will slash ~30% of their valuation during due diligence — and that this is preventable with proper preparation.

**Narrowest wedge: Sale Readiness Audit ($2-3K)**
- A diagnostic engagement (2-3 sessions over 1-2 weeks) where Vaiga identifies the specific issues a buyer will find and attack during due diligence
- Deliverable: a report listing the top 5-10 valuation risks, each with an estimated valuation impact (as % of enterprise value or dollar range), severity rating, and specific remediation steps with timeline
- Price: $2-3K (test with first 3 clients, adjust based on willingness to pay)
- Natural upsell into full "Exit Readiness Program" ($8-15K) — a 2-4 month engagement to implement the remediation roadmap, prepare the data room, and coach the founder through buyer interactions

**Pricing boundary:** The audit is diagnostic only ("here's what's wrong and what it'll cost you"). The full program is implementation ("we fix it together"). Clear separation prevents confusion.
